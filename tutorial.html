How to Play Checkers

  1. Objective: The goal is to capture all of your opponent's pieces or block them so they cannot move.
  2. Setup: The game is played on an 8x8 board. Each player starts with 12 pieces placed on the dark squares of the three rows closest to them.
  3. Moving Pieces: Players take turns moving one piece. Pieces can only move diagonally forward to an adjacent unoccupied square.
  4. Capturing: If an opponent's piece is adjacent to yours and the square beyond it is empty, you can jump over the opponent's piece and capture it.
  5. Becoming a King: When a piece reaches the opponent's back row, it is crowned and becomes a king, allowing it to move both forward and backward.
  6. Winning the Game: The game ends when one player captures all of the opponent's pieces or blocks them from making a legal move.
